I've built and delivered online learning experiences working directly alongside faculty and instructional designers. I also produced two substantial faculty-collaboration projects: a branching-narrative interactive video for a data science ethics course (Nobody's Fault), and a full update of an edX MOOC on smartphone engineering with a Cornell computer architecture faculty member.

I translate complex academic work for general audiences — and I've done it at scale. Over four years at Cornell, I produced 3–5 short documentary films per year profiling innovative teaching practice across the university: following Doug McKee's work in Economics, Natasha Holmes's physics education research, Gen Meredeth's design of Cornell's Master of Public Health curriculum, and others.
